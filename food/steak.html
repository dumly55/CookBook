New York Strip Steak

>
This is a delicious steak that is easy to make and tastes great. It is a great meal for a special occasion or just a nice dinner. We generally want to cut our strips here ranging from at least 1 inch to 1.5 inches at most. This will give you a nice medium rare steak. If you want it more well done, you can cut it thinner.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz New York Strip Cut
  • Seasoning Of Choice (like garlic salt)
  • 1 Sprig of Thyme
  • 1 Garlic Clove
  • 1 tbsp Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 1 tbsp Beef Tallow

Steps

  1. Take the 12 oz steak out of the fridge and let it sit at room temperature for 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat your grill or skillet to medium-high heat.
  3. Season the steak with your seasoning of choice on both sides.
  4. Add the olive oil to the skillet or pan.
  5. Once the oil is hot, add the steak to the skillet or grill.
  6. Cook for 3-5 minutes on one side, then flip and add in your 1 tbsp of beef tallow.
  7. Cook the flipped side for 3-5 minutes, ideally stopping after your internal temperature hits 10 degrees below your preferred temperature.
  8. Add the thyme and garlic clove to the skillet or pan during the last minute of cooking.
  9. Use a spoon to baste the steak with the melted beef tallow and juices from the pan.
  10. Check the internal temperature of the steak with a meat thermometer.
  11. Remove the steak from the heat and let it rest for 5-10 minutes before slicing.
  12. Slice against the grain and serve with your favorite sides.
